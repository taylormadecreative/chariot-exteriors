# PRODUCT.md — Chariot Exteriors

**Register:** brand (marketing website; the design is the product)

## What it is
Marketing website for **Chariot Exteriors**, a Dallas–Fort Worth home-exterior remodeling
company: **siding, windows, doors, and sunrooms**. Single source, locally owned, professional
installation. Primary conversion goal: get the visitor to request a **free in-home estimate**.

## Users
- Homeowners across the DFW metroplex, skewing **45–65**, often browsing on a **phone**.
- Researching a real, expensive home-improvement decision. They want to trust the company:
  established, local, accountable, quality work, fair process.
- Readability and obvious next steps matter more than cleverness. Larger type, high contrast.

## Brand voice
Three words: **sturdy, neighborly, premium.** Confident and warm, never corporate or salesy.
Talks like a trusted local pro, not a national chain. Concrete benefits, no hype words.
Banned: em dashes, "elevate / seamless / unleash / next-gen", fabricated stats.

## Strategic principles
- The "Get a Free Quote" path is everywhere: sticky header button, hero form, mid-page, footer.
- Show the work: real-looking project photos and before/after transformations carry the page.
- Trust scaffolding: locally owned, licensed & insured, free estimates, warranty, simple 1-2-3 process.
- Mobile-first, fast, accessible. Tap targets large, forms short.

## Anti-references
- Generic AI landing page (centered stack, icon-title-text cards, hero-metric template).
- Corporate big-box-chain coldness. Stock-y "two people shaking hands" clichés.
- Cramped tiny type. Anything that reads as a template.
